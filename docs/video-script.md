# Overdue - demo video script

Target length: 2:50. Hard cap is 3:00.
Format: screen recording with voiceover. No face cam needed.
Word count: about 410 words, which lands near 2:45 at a normal speaking pace. Do not rush it.

Record with a fresh daily model quota. The scripted dump in beat 2 is matched offline and fills the queue from the seed tasks, so only the escalation beat in 1:18 makes a live model call. Type the list exactly as written below.

---

## 0:00 - 0:18 | The problem

**Screen:** App open on the empty state. Just the headline and the dump bar. Still, no cursor movement.

**Voiceover:**
> Everyone keeps a version of this list. The refund you never claimed. The subscription still charging you. The deposit your landlord never sent back. You know exactly what to do. You just never do it, because someone has to go first, and the first message gets ignored on purpose.

---

## 0:18 - 0:48 | Dump to drafts

**Screen:** Type into the dump bar at a natural speed: `cancel gym, refund for the broken headphones, chase landlord about deposit, invoice from the March project, reschedule dentist`. Hit the button. Cut to the filled queue.

**Note:** start from a genuinely empty queue (clear site data if you have run it before), otherwise this falls through to a live model call.

**Voiceover:**
> So you dump it. Whatever messy words you have. Overdue pulls out each task, works out who it goes to and what you actually want, and writes the thing that gets it moving. An email, a call script, or a direct link. Five things I have been avoiding for months, ready in one pass.

---

## 0:48 - 1:18 | Open one, show the leverage

**Screen:** Click the headphones task. Review panel opens. Scroll slowly to the leverage block. Hover the source tag. Edit one field in the draft so it is visibly editable. Tap approve, mail client opens.

**Voiceover:**
> Open one and it is not just a polite note. It knows the rule. Defective goods, replacement or refund obligation, Consumer Protection Act. Every claim shows where it came from so you can check it, and nothing here is dressed up as legal advice. The draft is yours to edit. Then you tap once, and it goes.

---

## 1:18 - 1:52 | The escalation, on its own

**Screen:** Back to the queue, task now shows awaiting reply with a next-move date. Click the time-travel control, advance five days. The task flips to escalate, then the regenerated stage 2 draft appears. Open it and show the firmer wording side by side with the stage meter moving to two.

**Voiceover:**
> Here is the part that matters. Most of these do not fail on the first message. They fail because nobody follows up. So I advance the clock five days. No reply came back, so Overdue rewrites it, firmer, naming the delay, citing the regulation, and moves it up the ladder on its own. Polite, then firm, then formal. The clock is simulated. The engine underneath is real.

---

## 1:52 - 2:15 | The queue drains

**Screen:** Whole queue in view. Point out the state stamps. Mark one task resolved, let the recede animation play.

**Voiceover:**
> The queue is the product. You can see what is sent, what is waiting, what is due next. And when something finally lands, you mark it resolved and watch it go. That is the whole feeling I was building for.

---

## 2:15 - 2:30 | Safety, in one line

**Screen:** Slow pan across the review panel: the provenance tags, then the approve button.

**Voiceover:**
> One rule holds it together. The model proposes, deterministic code validates, and you authorize. Nothing sends by itself, nothing changes state by itself, and inferred details are labelled as inferred.

---

## 2:30 - 2:52 | Codex and GPT-5

**Screen:** Split or cut to the repo. Show the commit history scrolling, then the tests passing in the terminal.

**Voiceover:**
> GPT-5 does the extraction and the drafting, server side, with structured outputs and your text treated as data, never as instructions. And the build itself ran through Codex across one sustained session. It scaffolded the app, wrote the validation layer, the leverage rules and the escalation state machine test first, then the whole interface. Session ID is in the README.

## Recording notes

- Keep one backup take. If the live escalation call hits a rate limit mid-record, restart from Load demo and re-shoot only that beat.
- No copyrighted music. Silence or a royalty-free bed at low volume.
- No third-party logos on screen. If a real brand name shows in a task, rename it before recording.
- Upload public on YouTube, then paste the link into the Devpost form.
